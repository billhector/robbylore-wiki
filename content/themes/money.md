# Money

Money — specifically class translation — is the through-line of [[people/robby-hoffman|Robby]]'s comedy. She grew up in a Montreal apartment with **twelve people sharing one bathroom**, attended **a rich Jewish private school on subsidy**, left **a $32,500-a-year KPMG accounting job** for stand-up, and now talks openly about how she calibrates wealth in her marriage and her audience.

## Key Takeaways

- **Twelve people, one bathroom.** Roach and lice infestations. Cellophane on windows for heat retention. Brothers wearing hand-me-downs that Robby still references by name (*"my brother Manakim's pants"*).
- **Rich-Jewish-school-on-subsidy:** Robby got into a private Jewish Montreal school via interview; the principal offered to take just her, but grandfather insisted brothers come too. The brothers were out within a year.
- **KPMG salary:** $32,500/year as an accountant. She gave herself **six months** to break into comedy from there. See [[career/accounting-to-standup]].
- **First payday:** an HBO Canada option for $1,000 — she celebrated by buying one beer.
- **Dating-up:** [[bits/pull-rich-men|"How to pull rich men"]] is a literal Robby tutorial — she frames her Lubavitch + class background as the training set.
- **"I'm the last American dream."** — her closing line on [[projects/talk-easy|Talk Easy]] sits on top of this whole theme.
- **Tipping rant:** says tipping is a residue of racism and employers should just pay people.

## Why it matters

Robby's stand-up persona is *materially* class-aware in a way many comedians' isn't — she names dollar amounts, names jobs, names the precise mechanism of upward mobility. Her family is still in Montreal; she still flies coach alone; she still SuperCuts on Tuesdays.
