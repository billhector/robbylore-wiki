# Queerness

[[people/robby-hoffman|Robby]] is a butch-presenting lesbian. She came out as a teenager to her [[themes/orthodox-upbringing|Lubavitch mother]] — *"I have a girlfriend, she's blonde"* — and the mother's reply: *"Does she want to come for Shabbus?"*

## Key Takeaways

- **Came out at 17–18.** Was outed socially first; ran home and told her mother. Mother's initial reaction was warm; the homophobia returned later when mother became [[themes/orthodox-upbringing|born-again Christian]].
- **Has not identified as trans;** had [[career/top-surgery|top surgery]] in her early 30s and frames it as gender dysphoria, not transition. Joked about the [[bits/no-backsies-they-them|"no backsies" rule]] for they/them pronouns.
- **Butch identity is load-bearing** in her comedy. *"I am the boy in the relationship."*
- **["Testing God by being gay"]([[bits/testing-god-by-being-gay]])** — won't fly to Japan because she's already a queer woman married to another woman; doesn't want to push her luck with God.
- **"Pride float" demand** — she wants to lead the parade because *"I'm the Michael Jordan of this community — my wife had 25 boyfriends."*
- **"The queerest thing a queer girl could do right now is have a boyfriend."**
- Did first sex with a man at 21 — three times total.

## Why it matters

Robby's queerness reads as **proud, butch, and old-school** in a wave of comedy that's often softer or more identity-fluid. She talks about gay culture from a married-lesbian-with-a-house POV, not a coming-out POV.
