# Family

[[people/robby-hoffman|Robby]] is **the seventh of ten children**. Family is the structural fact under almost every other story she tells: religion, money, comedy, even her marriage.

## Key Takeaways

- **Ten kids.** Robby is #7 (per Guardian May 2026). Mother had all ten by age 30; the births spanned ~12.5 years — per Robby on Made It Out (Jul 2024) and Bein' Ian (Oct 2024), the siblings are clustered *"all about 13 months apart."* Robby has **three younger and six older** siblings.
- **Mother's morning:** woke at **5:30am** every day to cook, clean, care for the kids. "Emotionally absent" but "definitely physically present" (Guardian, May 2026).
- **Mother's tenacity by example.** Per Endless Honeymoon (Oct 2025): the grandfather babysat one evening a week so Robby's mother could take a night class at Concordia. She took **one class per semester for 12 years** and quietly graduated around the same time Robby finished her own three-year Montreal undergrad. *"She just by example was so tenacious."* Robby's frame: stability and a present mother were the load-bearing parenting; she **didn't need or get curation.**
- **Big Brother / Big Sister program ended early.** Robby had a Big Sister, **Sherry**, who lived with a male roommate **Stu** and would put on movies (Sherry would have been ~30; Robby loved her). One of Robby's brothers had a Big Brother who suggested an overnight camping trip — mother ended the program for the whole family: *"He's not going anywhere. You'll grow up without a father from here on out."* (Anniewood, Jul 2024)
- **The art-teacher anecdote (Endless Honeymoon, Oct 2025).** Around age 11, Robby was gifted at drawing. A teacher told her mother about a $45/year after-school art program. Mother's reply: *"We're fine with the art program just the way it is, thank you."* Mother was mad on the walk home. Robby now reads it as accidentally correct parenting — boredom and lack of curation are part of why she could keep herself company creatively.
- **Sister D'vorah recently graduated** (per Endless Honeymoon, Oct 2025): Robby threw a large graduation event; Kaya did not come. Robby treats sister participation in family events as voluntary — *"wherever they can meet us, we're just thrilled."*
- **Robby attended the Emmys (2025) with Kaya** (her oldest sister). Kaya walked Robby out after the loss.
- **Annual mother's-doctor-checkup ritual (per RIP Jordan Jensen, Mar 2026):** mother had *"reverence for doctors"* — once-a-year checkup before camp or school year. Mother would bring multiple kids at once; they were all weighed in their underwear. Brother **Menachem** was flagged underweight one year; mother (humiliated): *"Get dressed, get dressed. You don't weigh enough, embarrassing me, like we don't have food."*
- **Sister Khaya / Kaya's *"If the queen invited you for dinner"* line:** Robby's sibling-late-vs-early dynamic — Robby and Kaya are 15-minutes-early people; younger sisters are looser with time. Kaya's reproof: *"Would you be late if the queen invited you for dinner? No. Well, you don't even know the queen. I'm your sister. You're flesh and blood."*
- **Brother *"Shanaer"*** (corporate office, pseudonym/phonetic) — March 2023: complained to Robby about a Pride-month bulletin at work. *"The gay agenda is alive and well at work."* Robby: *"You know it's me. I'm a gay person."* His non-defense: *"Well, not you — you're my flesh and blood; I'm talking about [other gay people]."*
- **Brother Schmuly recommends a German Shepherd** for Robby + Gabby in LA (per Live From Bed, Aug 2025): *"You live in LA, it's a dangerous place. You got to get the dog."* Their actual dog: a Labradoodle. Robby's name for it: *"useless."*
- **Father quote on Robby's queerness (Anniewood, Jul 2024):** *"It's promiscuous behavior not befitting a daughter of Israel."*
- **Father estrangement:** Robby **has not spoken to her father since her early 20s**; he had already been mostly absent for years before that. The divorce + Montreal move happened "between the ages of five and 11" — Robby is hazy on exact timing.
- **The school-interview story:** see [[bits/package-deal|"It's a package deal"]]. Three brothers got into a fistfight during the interview; school offered Robby the scholarship alone; grandfather refused. The boys lasted a year before being expelled. Robby graduated.
- **Father:** American, charming, alcoholic, religious, abusive, mostly absent. Once left young Robby at a gas station after a candy stop. Died long after the divorce. Instead of paying child support after the split, **he bought himself a used Porsche** for ~$10,000 while the family was on welfare; mailed Robby's mother a picture of the car. Robby on inheriting his car-eye anyway: *"When I look into the rear-view mirror, I see his eyes in my eyes. It helps me have a relationship with him, enjoying something he enjoys. It's not ideal, but it's not nothing."* She drives a Porsche 911 today.
- **Mother:** Born in Montreal, became Lubavitch as a young adult in the 1970s, later became born-again Christian. Fled the father in Florida with the kids. *"A crazy reader."* Taught Robby the [[themes/money|"important to give, to remind yourself you're in a position to give"]] lesson.
- **Grandfather [[people/nathan-letovsky|Nathan Letovsky]]** — Canadian, painter, post-war butcher. The painting collection that hung "wherever there was a square inch spare" on his visits became the only objects Robby grabbed from her house during the LA-fire evacuation.
- **The escape:** [[career/montreal-flight|Grandfather Zadie + his French-Canadian best friend Germaine arrived in Florida with two cabs]]; flew the family to Montreal in February; [[people/uncle-eddie|great-uncle Eddie]] picked them up; the temporary stay became permanent.
- **The Hoffman siblings:** see [[people/robby-hoffman#Family|the Robby page]] for the full named list — Kaya, Schmuly, Manakim, the color-blind FedEx brother, Devorah/D'vorah, Y'hudis, Chaya, and others.
- **Niece Teddy** is named after [[people/uncle-eddie|Uncle Eddie]].
- The family found out about Robby's career in TV one project at a time; her brother Schmuly's biggest excitement was [[people/steve-carell|Steve Carell]] from The Office and [[people/tim-dylan|Tim Dylan]] from comedy.
- Robby and Gabby host her brother in their LA house. Gabby's mother helps with the house.

## Why it matters

- Robby's comedy is constantly running family count: number of kids, number of bathrooms, number of relatives still calling her Rifka.
- The family is split between two affective registers: the Montreal-Orthodox cluster (mother, brothers, Uncle Eddie) and the Robby/Kaya migration outward to LA / Arizona / NY.
