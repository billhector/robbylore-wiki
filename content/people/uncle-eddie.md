# Uncle Eddie (Great-Uncle)

**"Zadie Eddie."** Brother of [[people/robby-hoffman|Robby]]'s grandfather. The Hoffman family folk hero — the great-uncle who took in [[themes/family|Robby's mother and the ten kids]] in Montreal on a moment's notice when they fled Robby's father in Florida.

## Key Takeaways

- **Took in the whole family.** Robby's mother, the ten children, and what came with them.
- **Lived in a basement**, where he eventually died (in his 70s or 80s).
- **Diabetic.** "Bole" is the family endearment for him.
- **Memorably described:** "Used to piss all over his pants walking around" (per Neal Brennan podcast retelling).
- **Niece Teddy** — Robby's niece — is named after him.
- **Painted by Robby's grandfather Zadie Nathan,** who won a trip to Paris in a painting contest and made his brother Eddie the subject. The Hoffman family still has the painting.
- **Dropped Robby's birthday cake** once. Joked-about, never recovered.
- **Friends? "Friends is for kids."** Robby quotes Eddie on this.
- **Robby's little sister Y'hudis** was particularly close to him.

## Why it matters

Uncle Eddie is the structural fact that allowed Robby's mother to leave the father in Florida — without him, the Hoffman family's Montreal life doesn't happen, and Robby doesn't end up the comedian she is.
