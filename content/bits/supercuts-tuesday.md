# Supercuts Tuesday

Recurring [[people/robby-hoffman|Robby]] **frugality bit** — she gets her hair cut at **Supercuts on Tuesday** because Tuesday is the discount day. The bit sits alongside [[bits/cost-per-wear|cost-per-wear]] watch math and [[bits/multiples-of-18|multiples-of-18]] negotiation as instances of *"I have money now but the brain doesn't update."*

## Key Takeaways

- **The frugality is genuine; the comedy is in the detail.** The Supercuts choice is real — Robby genuinely uses the chain, genuinely goes on the discount day.
- **Hair-and-glasses constancy:** the bit is a sibling to Robby's *"glasses + hair: both unchanged for 10+ years"* policy. The Supercuts choice is part of how she keeps the look stable.
- **Class-aware status display:** Robby uses Supercuts Tuesday to signal that her wealth (Rolex, Porsche, writer's cabin) hasn't translated into discretionary lifestyle spending — the wealth went into durable artifacts, not into the daily-spend pattern.
- **Frequent ammo against agent's-side-LA culture** — Robby contrasts her Supercuts Tuesday with the Beverly Hills $400 salon as a status-inversion gesture.
- **Pairs with [[bits/polo-lounge|The Polo Lounge $7 beer]]** as the early-LA-frugality bit cluster.
