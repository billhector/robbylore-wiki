# "It's a Package Deal"

A [[people/robby-hoffman|Robby]] family story she returns to. At an interview for a Jewish private school in Montreal, her three brothers got into a fistfight in the room over a chair. The principal pulled the boys out. After Robby sold herself hard (*"They're animals. I don't know what to tell you. I think I could be very good at this school."*) the school offered the scholarship spot **only to her, not the brothers.** Her grandfather **[[people/nathan-letovsky|Zadie]]** refused.

## Key Takeaways

- Robby's brothers fistfought during the interview; school offered the scholarship to Robby alone.
- Grandfather Zadie: *"No — it's a package deal."*
- The boys lasted a year before being expelled; Robby graduated through.
- A founding Zadie-the-grandfather hero beat, alongside the [[career/montreal-flight|Montreal flight]].

## The line

- **Principal:** *"We'll take the girl, but not the boys."*
- **Grandfather:** *"No — it's a package deal."*

## What happened

- The boys lasted maybe a year at the school before being kicked out.
- **Robby stayed** and ended up the only sibling who graduated through.
- She still calls herself a "troublemaker" in that period — clever, but the kind who'd argue *"this is an injustice!"* over a pop quiz.

## Why it matters

- Encapsulates the Hoffman-family ethic: nobody gets pulled out alone.
- Sits alongside [[bits/proposing-via-crossword|the crossword proposal]] and [[career/montreal-flight|the Montreal flight]] as one of the founding Zadie-the-grandfather hero beats.
