# Trash Can Rant

A small-grievances classic that has appeared at least once at length, in Mal Glowenke's "U-Haul or You Ghost" game on the Made It Out podcast (July 2024).

## Key Takeaways

- **Bathroom trash can is the only acceptable trash can** — most household refuse routes through there.
- **Pedal / kick-lid lids fail** — leaving you *"lifting and tucking"* every day.
- **The despair vector:** lifting-and-tucking over years contributes to mental-health collapse. Robby ties bin-mechanism to suicidal ideation.
- **Correct configuration:** open-concept can, emptied regularly.
- **Practical corollary:** if you U-Haul with Robby, she handles the bin.

## The bit

When asked whether she'd date a woman who refused to keep a trash can in her bathroom, [[people/robby-hoffman|Robby]] launches into a several-minute philosophical case:

- The bathroom trash can is the only acceptable trash can — Pantene bottles, makeup remover, used floss don't go anywhere else.
- The **pedal / kick-lid** model is *"the worst feeling you could have."* The mechanism breaks; you're left *"lifting and tucking"* — manually raising the lid, dropping the trash, tucking the bag back.
- This is *"the kind of day-and-day-out activity that brings a person to a Brink."* Robby's escalation: lifting and tucking, over years, is one of the slow-acting reasons people *"jump in front of the train."* You can trace despair back to the broken kick-lid.
- The only correct configuration: **open-concept trash can, emptied regularly,** so that throwing something away is *"satisfying"* and not *"like trying to dangle it onto an empty bottle of Pantene Pro-V."*

## Why it lands

The bit is a perfect example of Robby's [[people/robby-hoffman#Operating posture (running quote bank)|"small grievances"]] style — a single domestic-physics observation expanded into a quality-of-life thesis with mortal stakes. It also crystallizes her "if you live with me, I'll handle the chores" frame: she U-Hauls *and* she empties the bin.
