# "I don't take no bait"

Title bit from [[people/robby-hoffman|Robby]]'s appearance on Whitney Cummings' *Good For You* podcast (EP #240). The frame: **refusing to give the provocateur the reaction they're fishing for** is a form of grace and composure — Robby treats not-reacting as a learned discipline rather than a personality default.

## Key Takeaways

- **Core frame:** when someone is baiting you, the win condition is to **not bite**. Robby treats this as practical wisdom rather than therapy-speak.
- **Gender + grace overlay:** the bit lands harder because it pulls from Robby's [[themes/queerness|queer-Orthodox-Brooklyn]] background — she names how she watched her mother absorb decades of bait without flinching, and frames the move as something she inherited.
- **Application to comedy work:** Robby extends the principle to hecklers, online haters, and especially to bait-y questions from interviewers — *"Whatever, I literally do not give a fuck."*
- **Tension with the *"Jihad against semantics"* pattern:** Robby will *sometimes* break the no-bait rule when the bait is structural (poverty, kosher, "trans-discourse as poverty-distraction") — *those* she'll engage with deliberately. See [[people/jordan-jensen|Jordan Jensen]]'s Bein' Ian Ep. 115 for the "Jihad" framing in full.
- **Connected to:** the [[themes/dating]] no-ghost-but-still-leave ethos, and the [[bits/trash-can-rant|small-grievances]] aesthetic where you channel annoyance through detail rather than reaction.
