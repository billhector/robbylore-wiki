# The Degrassi Audition That Didn't Happen

Around **age 12–14**, [[people/robby-hoffman|Robby]] was scouted at her Montreal acting school for a red-headed teen role in a Canadian TV show. The role turned out to be on ***Degrassi***. Robby's mother killed the audition.

## Key Takeaways

- Robby was scouted for a *Degrassi* role at age 12-14; the role went to [[people/stacy-farber|Stacy Farber]].
- The scout asked for $200 of head shots and wanted Robby driven 5 hours to Toronto — her mother heard "predator" and shut it down.
- Counter-example to the "emotionally absent mother" framing — she was physically present and protective when it mattered.

## What happened

- A casting scout came through Robby's school in Montreal looking for a red-headed character.
- Robby's drama teacher recommended her; she had no acting experience but had been in one school play.
- She did the initial audition. The casting team wanted her to come to **Toronto** (a 5-hour drive) for a callback.
- The scout called Robby's house and spoke to her **mother**.
- He asked for **head shots — $200 worth.**
- Her mother heard "grown man wants to take my daughter to Toronto and asked for $200 of pictures" and shut it down: *"You're not going nowhere. You're staying here."*
- The role went to **[[people/stacy-farber|Stacy Farber]]**, who Robby met years later and describes as "lovely."

## Why it matters

- Frames Robby's mother as protective in a clear, unambiguous way — non-frilly parenting that nonetheless prevented a potentially exploitative situation.
- Robby on Q with Tom Power: *"By the way, she's not wrong. We've had so many documentaries come out in later years since of all kinds of be suspicious of child actor."*
- A small counter-example to the "emotionally absent" framing of her mother — she was physically present and protective when it mattered.
