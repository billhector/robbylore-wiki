# Top Surgery

[[people/robby-hoffman|Robby]] had **top surgery (double mastectomy)** in her early 30s. She tells the story most fully on [[projects/blocks|Neal Brennan's Blocks]] podcast — including the two-year deliberation it took to get there.

## Key Takeaways

- **Gender dysphoria, not transition.** Robby has not identified as trans. She frames the surgery the way some women frame breast-augmentation surgery — *"some girls want bigger tits"* — as bodywork that fixed a dysphoric feeling.
- **Distinct from BRCA cancer surgery.** Robby is careful to draw the line: this is plastic surgery for gender reasons, not the cancer-prevention surgery many cis women have.
- **["Two-year rule"]([[bits/two-year-decision-rule]]):** Robby waited two years between deciding she wanted the surgery and getting it. Tattoos get a one-year wait; permanent body decisions get two.
- **Gabby's analogy** for explaining it: *"Some girls want bigger tits."*
- **The regret-symmetry argument (Anniewood Ep. 20, Mar 2023):** *"Either way you have regret. The only thing I knew was when I had them it wasn't great. So once I cut them off and I don't like it, it's not great — it's the same. I end up in the same place. I have a chance of liking it, something I don't know."*
- **3% breast tissue left.** Surgeon deliberately did not go fully concave. *"They don't leave anything when it's BRCA — you'd get reconstructive. I'm not concave. The skin is taut, but it's there."*
- **Recovery:** first stand-up show ~3 weeks post-op.
- **"No goof, no test."** Robby was the first person she told and the only person she consulted: *"No one put no goof on me — treated myself."*
- **Don't buy surgery on sale.** Robby's running advice when asked by listeners looking for cheaper top-surgery options: *"You don't buy your surgery on sale. You don't buy a parachute on sale."* (Anniewood Ep. 20)
- **Insurance hot-take (per Bein' Ian, Oct 2024):** the trans-discourse hostility comes partly from people not wanting taxes/insurance to cover what they read as elective surgery; Robby's reply — *"we paid Wall Street when they crashed it from our pocket; that is way more money than 20 trans-care surgeries."*

## Why it matters

The surgery is one of the few topics Robby reserves for long-form podcast space rather than late-night clips. It anchors her queerness without forcing her into the "trans" label that has, in 2024–2026, become contested in comedy and elsewhere.
