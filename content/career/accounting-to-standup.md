# Accounting to Stand-Up

[[people/robby-hoffman|Robby]] worked at **KPMG** as an accountant for **$32,500 per year**. She gave herself **six months** to break into comedy from the day she quit. The bet paid off via a blind-packet submission to [[projects/the-chris-gethard-show|The Chris Gethard Show]] titled *"AIDS Russian Roulette."*

## Key Takeaways

- **KPMG salary:** $32,500. Robby names the number in every telling.
- **Self-imposed runway:** six months.
- **First plank:** the [[projects/the-chris-gethard-show|Gethard]] blind packet.
- **Stole the laptop on the way out** of KPMG. (Per Not Skinny But Not Fat.)
- The accounting background is also why she did her own [[career/hbo-script-sneak|HBO script sneak]] using Word + Courier 12 instead of paying for screenwriting software — the bookkeeper's instinct, in her telling, never left.
- **The choice was explicit class-escape:** *"As a kid I knew one thing: I gotta get out of here. When I got to school, I was like, 'What's the least amount of work, the most amount of payoff?' So I went into accounting and worked at KPMG. Had I stayed there, I would have made partner. I was determined not to be poor."* (FT 2026)
- **School:** **[[themes/canada|McGill]] University** — **accounting + communications** double major (per Wikipedia). Robby chose it explicitly because **the program comes with a free laptop**. *"You get a free laptop. You get a couple of bucks guaranteed. And then when you want to do comedy, you say, 'What's the best place to bomb?'"*
- **Post-undergrad: McGill graduate CPA program.** Robby enrolled, then **quit a few hours into her first day** to pursue comedy (per Forbes, 24 July 2019). The CPA-program-quit is the *real* comedy-pivot moment; the **KPMG audit job** that the wiki has emphasized for the $32,500 + six-months runway came *before* this — the order is: KPMG → quit comedy attempt → re-enrolled CPA grad program → quit hours in → comedy for good. This distinction matters because the Forbes interview cites the CPA walkout as the watershed; the podcast retellings emphasize KPMG salary math.
- **Applied only to McGill** for undergrad — *"the application was cheaper than any of the other options I was considering."* (Forbes, 2019)
- **Karl Moore connection:** the Forbes profile is written by Karl Moore, Robby's former McGill **MBA leadership** professor. Moore: *"some decade ago she took one of my classes and to this day I still remember her presentation as standing out among that of her cohort and there being a great deal of laughter."*
- **The first written show was not picked up** but the writing got Robby noticed in the industry — leading to PBS picking her up for [[projects/odd-squad|Odd Squad]] (per Forbes 2019).
- **Day-shift / night-shift framing** (Forbes, 2019): *"I would go to a show and be there from 10 to 7, then go home for two hours for supper, and then I'd hit the stage at 9 in the evening."* Robby was *"the last to leave the writer's room and the first to arrive at her evening gigs."*
- **"Nomad" self-framing:** *"I feel most at home when the lights are on me and I'm dancing around on stage."* (Forbes, 2019).
- **Operating quotes from 2019:**
  - *"I'm not somebody who would be a starving artist. I don't enjoy being poor, I like to sleep, I like not to have those worries."*
  - *"You have to listen for your passion. I was lucky that I found it when I did. My passion found me, but I didn't want it to happen."*
  - *"I wake up and I love where I work. I have never missed a day or an hour of work."*
  - **Tour-guide framing of stand-up:** *"I can really bring the audience somewhere, like a tour guide. Hopefully the audience forgets about what their world is and they go somewhere completely different with me."*
  - **On the first 10 seconds of a set:** *"The first time I see the audience is when I step out on stage. In those ten seconds I have read so much information."*
- **First standup venue:** **George Braithwaite's loft** in Montreal — *"horrendous but also very cool at the same time… raw lofts… he lived with an artist who painted massive paintings."* Robby brought two high-school friends on a December night. Five jokes about her family.
- **Robby's initial agent-management posture** (Q with Tom Power): she **refused 1-week and 2-week writers'-room gigs** because she wouldn't leave her stable KPMG paycheck for short contracts. The [[projects/odd-squad|Odd Squad]] gig — six months as writers' assistant — was the first thing long enough for her to say yes.
- **"Comedy was foisted upon me, like Moses or something"** (Guardian, May 2026) — her preferred framing of the calling.
- **Discovery vector:** Robby was *not* a comedy fan growing up. The family was funny — mother and brother especially — but stand-up was not a category she knew. She discovered it as an adult through **Eddie Murphy**: *"I had the luxury of starting stand-up while discovering it, so I was like, watching Eddie Murphy, thinking, 'Oh, my God, somebody should do something with this!'"* (Bustle, Oct 2023)
- **The calling-as-curse framing:** *"This is what I'm supposed to be doing. I wouldn't wish a calling on my worst enemy; it truly is a nightmare. If you can do anything else, do it. I never wanted to do stand-up."* (Bustle, Oct 2023)

## Why it matters

- Robby anchors her credibility on stage and in podcasts on the *real* career switch — not "I always knew" but "I quit a real job at a Big Four firm and gave myself six months."
- The KPMG period is the bridge between [[themes/orthodox-upbringing|Lubavitch Montreal]] and the TV career — the years of legibility before the comedy career took.
