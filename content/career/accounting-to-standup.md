# Accounting to Stand-Up

[[people/robby-hoffman|Robby]] worked at **KPMG** as an accountant for **$32,500 per year**. She gave herself **six months** to break into comedy from the day she quit. The bet paid off via a blind-packet submission to [[projects/the-chris-gethard-show|The Chris Gethard Show]] titled *"AIDS Russian Roulette."*

## Key Takeaways

- **KPMG salary:** $32,500. Robby names the number in every telling.
- **Self-imposed runway:** six months.
- **First plank:** the [[projects/the-chris-gethard-show|Gethard]] blind packet.
- **Stole the laptop on the way out** of KPMG. (Per Not Skinny But Not Fat.)
- The accounting background is also why she did her own [[career/hbo-script-sneak|HBO script sneak]] using Word + Courier 12 instead of paying for screenwriting software — the bookkeeper's instinct, in her telling, never left.
- **The choice was explicit class-escape:** *"As a kid I knew one thing: I gotta get out of here. When I got to school, I was like, 'What's the least amount of work, the most amount of payoff?' So I went into accounting and worked at KPMG. Had I stayed there, I would have made partner. I was determined not to be poor."* (FT 2026)
- **School:** **[[themes/canada|McGill]] University** — accounting. Robby chose it explicitly because **the program comes with a free laptop**. *"You get a free laptop. You get a couple of bucks guaranteed. And then when you want to do comedy, you say, 'What's the best place to bomb?'"*
- **First standup venue:** **George Braithwaite's loft** in Montreal — *"horrendous but also very cool at the same time… raw lofts… he lived with an artist who painted massive paintings."* Robby brought two high-school friends on a December night. Five jokes about her family.
- **Robby's initial agent-management posture** (Q with Tom Power): she **refused 1-week and 2-week writers'-room gigs** because she wouldn't leave her stable KPMG paycheck for short contracts. The [[projects/odd-squad|Odd Squad]] gig — six months as writers' assistant — was the first thing long enough for her to say yes.
- **"Comedy was foisted upon me, like Moses or something"** (Guardian, May 2026) — her preferred framing of the calling.

## Why it matters

- Robby anchors her credibility on stage and in podcasts on the *real* career switch — not "I always knew" but "I quit a real job at a Big Four firm and gave myself six months."
- The KPMG period is the bridge between [[themes/orthodox-upbringing|Lubavitch Montreal]] and the TV career — the years of legibility before the comedy career took.
